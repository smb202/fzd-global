— Common business problems

What financial services and insurance leaders bring to us.

Recurring patterns we see in insurance, banking and asset management. Regulatory complexity is high; reporting must be accurate, auditable and fast.

Problems we solve

  • Regulatory reporting complexity (Solvency, IFRS)
  • Fragmented customer and transaction data
  • Manual risk and compliance reporting
  • Legacy BI tools at end of life
  • Slow month- and quarter-end close
  • Inconsistent KPIs between actuarial and finance

Solutions we build

  • Governed reporting layer with lineage
  • Compliance and regulatory dashboards
  • Data warehouse modernization
  • KPI governance & data quality rules
  • Secure analytics with role-based access
  • CFO and FP&A cockpits

Example KPIs

  • Combined ratio, claims ratio
  • Solvency Capital Requirement (SCR)
  • Customer lifetime value
  • Fraud detection rate, claim cycle time
  • IFRS reporting close time
  • Cost-to-income ratio

Selected work in Financial Services & Insurance.

Insurance · 2024–2025

200 dashboards migrated from Power BI to Grafana

SQL · Grafana API · Power Automate · SharePoint

Migrated 200 Power BI dashboards to Grafana. Created CIO-level dashboards. Enabled dynamic parameterization, auto-refresh and row-level security. Conducted training and workshops across departments.

Operational dashboards moved off licensed BI for cost savings. CIO visibility unified.
Insurance · 2024–Present

Project portfolio automation

Power Automate · Power Apps · MS Lists · SharePoint

Automated project portfolio management processes. Power Automate flows for status tracking across <100 departments. Power Automate App for project submissions. Modernized data collection with MS Lists.

PMO admin time cut roughly in half across the participating departments.
Insurance · 2019

Insurance analytics platform with ML

Python · scikit-learn · Power BI

Developed an insurance analytics platform. Built predictive customer segmentation models in Jupyter Notebooks. Designed dashboards and held workshops with the sales department.

Customer segmentation moved from intuition-driven to model-driven.
